\section{Network and Data-Flow Governance}
\label{sec:network-requirements}

\subsection{Principles for School Pilots}

Network planning must enforce \textbf{local processing}, \textbf{least privilege}, and
\textbf{no raw-media egress}:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item sensing and zone processing remain on institutionally controlled networks;
  \item staff decision-support interfaces are reachable only from authorized subnets;
  \item operational data flows stay on-premises except for explicitly approved research exports;
  \item no Internet pathway is provisioned for raw video or audio from DUALEXIS components.
\end{itemize}

These principles align with the DPIA checklist (Section~\ref{sec:dpia}) and operational
degraded-mode requirements (Section~\ref{sec:operational-protocols}).

\subsection{Continuity and Degraded Mode}

Institutions must document \textbf{manual emergency protocols} when digital coordination
is unavailable.
Pilot planning should agree:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item who declares degraded mode (safety lead + IT);
  \item how staff revert to radio, PA, and existing SMS procedures;
  \item recovery time objectives appropriate to educational operations (indicative MVP:
        same-day restoration of coordination service where feasible);
  \item backup of audit and coordination records per retention policy.
\end{itemize}

Single-site MVP may use a simplified coordination footprint; multi-site extensions
should plan higher availability with school IT.

\subsection{IT Planning Checklist (Non-Technical Summary)}

Before go-live, school IT and the steering committee should confirm:

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
  \item trust-zone diagram approved by DPO;
  \item no raw-media egress paths in firewall policy;
  \item time synchronization across site components (for coherent event ordering);
  \item encrypted staff access to decision-support interfaces;
  \item certificate and access-review procedures in operational runbooks;
  \item degraded-mode runbook exercised in tabletop drill.
\end{enumerate}

Detailed broker configuration, subject namespaces, port lists, and reference firewall
matrices appear in Appendix~\ref{app:technical-architecture}.
