\section{School and Institutional Context}
\label{sec:school-context}

\subsection{Target Environments}

DUALEXIS pilots target \textbf{confined educational spaces} where duty-of-care, occupant
density, and egress constraints create safety-critical situational awareness needs:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item primary and secondary schools;
  \item multi-building campuses with shared circulation zones;
  \item SEK/UCJC-affiliated sites and partner schools in structured research collaborations;
  \item indoor zones: hallways, stairwells, cafeterias, assembly areas, near exits.
\end{itemize}

Outdoor perimeters, sports fields, and public streets are \textbf{out of scope} for initial
pilots unless explicitly approved with revised zone taxonomy and governance review.

\subsection{Alignment with Educational Values}

School communities require monitoring approaches that:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item minimize student data exposure and avoid surveillance normalization;
  \item preserve dignity and trust through transparency and purpose limitation;
  \item keep human authority over consequential decisions;
  \item document audit trails for accountability without punitive automation.
\end{itemize}

DUALEXIS requirements reflect these constraints as \textbf{design invariants}, not
optional configuration toggles.

\subsection{Horizon Europe and Consortium Context}

For Horizon Europe and similar consortia, this document provides a \textbf{common deployment
requirements baseline} that partner schools can adapt locally.
It does not substitute site-specific legal review or national authority guidance.

Partner institutions should harmonize:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item semantic event schemas and zone identifiers;
  \item evaluation metrics and anonymized reporting formats;
  \item governance templates (DPIA checklist, training outlines);
  \item optional federated research metrics export (disabled by default).
\end{itemize}
