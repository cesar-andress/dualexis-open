\subsection{School-controlled operation (plain language)}

Each participating site uses \textbf{school-controlled equipment and accounts}.
Four operational roles apply:

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
  \item \textbf{Zone equipment} --- approved sensors process information locally; raw video/audio
        are not kept as recordings by default.
  \item \textbf{Site coordination} --- combines zone summaries, maintains review queues, and stores
        audit records (structured text only).
  \item \textbf{Staff review screen} --- trained staff see advisories and record decisions;
        \textbf{advisory only}.
  \item \textbf{Optional research export} (off by default) --- anonymized metrics only; no operational control.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Critical rule:} persistent records are \textbf{structured zone summaries and audit logs}---not
video or audio files.
Technical detail for IT teams: Appendix~\ref{app:technical-architecture}.

\subsection{Sensing scope (minimize, do not maximize)}

Coverage follows the \textbf{co-designed zone list}---not ``camera everywhere'' deployment.

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering
  \caption{Planning tiers (illustrative; not a product catalogue).}
  \label{tab:capability-tiers}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{@{}p{2.6cm}p{4.2cm}p{4.8cm}@{}}
    \toprule
    Tier & Typical use & Note \\
    \midrule
    Light & Audio / environmental sensors & Small pilots \\
    Standard & One approved stream per zone & Most MVP sites \\
    Enhanced & Busy zones needing redundancy & Requires IT capacity review \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
  \item sensors observe \textbf{spaces (zones)}, not named individuals;
  \item signage and parent/staff notices align with the DPIA (Appendix~\ref{app:governance-templates});
  \item coordination server stays on school premises with access controls and backup;
  \item right-size to protocol needs agreed in the protocol-mapping workshop.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Degraded mode}

If coordination services fail: safety lead and IT declare degraded mode; staff follow existing
radio, PA, and emergency procedures; no cloud failover without steering-committee approval.
